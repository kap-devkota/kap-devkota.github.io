Important Vector Field Proofs

1. General Tensor Properties and Lie Derivatives

1.1: Given a vector field \(X=X^{i}\partial_i\) and a function sequence \(\{f_1,f_2 \ldots\}\) which uniformly converges to \(f\). Then, if \(|X^i| \lt M\) for some \(M \lt \infty\), then \(\lim_{t\rightarrow \infty} X(f_t) = X(f)\)

1.2: Given \(X\) a vector field generated by a flow \(F_t\) on a manifold \(M\). Then, \((F_{-t})_* Y_{F_t(p)} \in T_pM\). The first order component of the Taylor expansion of \((F_{-t})_* Y_{F_t(p)} \in T_pM\) constitutes the lie derivative of \(Y\). In other words, \begin{align} L_X(Y)_p = [X, Y]_p = \lim_{t\rightarrow 0}\frac{(F_{-t})_*Y_{F_t(p)} - Y_{p}}{t} \end{align}

1.2: For covariant tensors \(T\) of type \((0, k)\), the Lie derivative \(L_X T\) becomes \begin{align} (L_{X}T)_p (Y_1, \ldots, Y_k) &= \lim_{t\rightarrow 0}\frac{(F^*_t T)_p(Y_1, \ldots, Y_k) - T_p(Y_1, \ldots, Y_k)}{t} \\ &= \lim_{t\rightarrow 0}\frac{(T)_{F(p)}((F_t)_*{Y_1} , \ldots, (F_t)_*{Y_k}) - T_p(Y_1, \ldots, Y_k)}{t} \\ &= \lim_{t\rightarrow 0} -\sum_{i} T_{p}(Y_1, \ldots, L_X(Y)) + D_X(T_p(Y_1, \ldots, Y_k)) \end{align}

1.3: \((L_XL)_YT = [L_X, L_Y]T - L_{[X,Y]}T = 0\) \begin{align} (L_XL)_YT(Z_1, \ldots, Z_k) &= L_XL_YT(Z_1, \ldots, Z_k) - L_YL_X T(Z_1, \ldots, Z_k) - L_{[X,Y]}T(Z_1, \ldots, Z_k) \\ \text{func. component}: & \\ &= D_X (D_Y(T(Z_1, \ldots, Z_k))) - D_Y (D_X(T(Z_1, \ldots, Z_k))) \\ &\quad - D_{[X, Y]}(T(Z_1, \ldots, Z_k)) \\ &= A-B - (A-B) = 0 \\ \text{lie component at } Z_i, Z_j, i\neq j: & \\ &= T(Z_1, \ldots L_XZ_i, \ldots L_YZ_j, \ldots) - T(Z_1, \ldots L_XZ_i, \ldots L_YZ_j, \ldots) \\ &\quad - 0, \text{ as the last component acts on the same VF} \\ &= 0 \\ \text{lie component at the same VF } Z_i: \\ &= T(Z_1, \ldots, L_XL_Y Z_i, \ldots) - T(Z_1, \ldots, L_YL_X Z_i, \ldots) \\ &\quad - T(Z_1, \ldots, L_{[X, Y]}Z_i, \ldots) \\ &= A-B-(A-B)=0 \end{align} So, 1.3 is satisfied.

1.4 Hessian of a function on a manifold \begin{align} Hess(f)(X, Y) = \frac{1}{2} (L_{\nabla f }g)(X, Y) \end{align} Where \(\nabla f\) is the gradient of \(f\): \begin{align} (\nabla f, X) &= X^{i} \partial_j f \\ \therefore \nabla f &= g^{ij} \partial_i f \partial_j \end{align}

1.5 Divergence definition using Lie algebra
The original definition of divergence is \begin{align} L_X vol = (div X) vol \end{align} Select a positively oriented orthogonal frame \(\{E_1, \ldots E_n\}\). Then \begin{align} (L_X vol) (E_1, \ldots, E_n) &= - \sum_i vol(E_1, \ldots, L_X E_i, \ldots) + 0 \\ &= -\sum_i g(E_i, L_X E_i) \\ &= 1/2 \sum_i (L_X g) (E_i, E_i) \\ \end{align}

1.6 Similarly, the laplacian \(\Delta f\) is defined as \begin{align} \Delta f &= div \nabla f \\ &= 1/2 \sum_i L_{\nabla f} (E_i, E_i) \\ &= 1/2 Trace(Hessian(f)) \end{align}

2. Lie Groups

Lie Groups are Riemannian manifolds with group property. It allows a natural automorphism: \(Ad_{h}: x \rightarrow h x h^{-1}\).

2.1 If a vf \(X\) in a Lie Group is left-invariant, then the flow \(F_t\) of \(X\) at origin \(e\) is \(F_t(e)=\exp{(tX)}\) \begin{align} \lim_{t\rightarrow 0} \frac{F_{t}(p) - p}{t} = X_{p} \end{align}

\begin{align} Hess(f)(X, Y) &= \frac{1}{2}(L_{\nabla f} g)(X, Y) \\ &= \frac{1}{2}(L_{\nabla f} (g(X, Y)) - g(L_{\nabla f}(X), Y) - g(X, L_{\nabla f}(Y))) \\ &= \frac{1}{2}(g(\nabla_{\nabla f}X, Y) + g(X, \nabla_{\nabla f}Y) - g(L_{\nabla f}(X), Y) - g(X, L_{\nabla f}(Y))) \\ &= \frac{1}{2}(g(\nabla_X \nabla f, Y) + g(X, \nabla_Y \nabla f)) \tag{B}\\ &= \frac{1}{2} (D_X(Y(f)) + D_Y(X(f)) - (\nabla_XY)(f) - (\nabla_YX)(f)) \\ &= \frac{1}{2} (X(Y(f)) + Y(X(f)) - (\nabla_X Y)(f) - (\nabla_XY(f) + Y(X(f)) - X(Y(f)))) \\ &= X(Y(f)) - (\nabla_X Y)(f) \end{align} (B) can also be written as \begin{align} g(\nabla_X \nabla f, Y) - g(X, \nabla_Y \nabla f)&= D_X(Y(f)) - D_Y(X(f)) - g(\nabla f, \nabla_X Y) + g(\nabla f, \nabla_Y X) \\ &= g([X, Y], \nabla f) - g(\nabla_XY, \nabla f) + g(\nabla_YX, \nabla f) \\ &= 0 \end{align}

Reimannian curvature tensor

\begin{align} R(X, Y)Z = \nabla_X\nabla_Y Z - \nabla_Y \nabla_X Z - \nabla_{[X, Y]}Z \end{align} Curvature tensor can be represented as \begin{align} {R_{ij}}^l_{\ k} &= \partial_i \Gamma^{l}_{jk} - \partial_j \Gamma^{l}_{ik} + \Gamma_{jk}^s\Gamma_{is}^l - \Gamma_{ik}^s \Gamma_{js}^l \\ R_{ijkl} &= R_{ks} {R_{ij}}^{s}_{\ l} \end{align} Proofs:

Symmetry

\begin{align} 1.\quad R_{ijkl} &= -R_{jikl} & \text{Self evident} \\ 2.\quad R_{ijkl} &= -R_{ijlk} \\ R_{ijkl} &= (\nabla_{e_i} \nabla_{e_j} e_k - \nabla_{e_j} \nabla_{e_i} e_k, e_l) \\ &= \partial_i \partial_j (g(e_k, e_l)) - \partial_j \partial_i (g(e_k, e_l)) - (e_k, \nabla_{e_i} \nabla_{e_j}e_l -\nabla_{e_j} \nabla_{e_i} e_l) \\ &= -R_{ijlk}\\ 3.\quad R_{ijkl} &= R_{klij} \\ R_{ijkl} &= (\nabla_{e_i} \nabla_{e_k} e_j - \nabla_{e_j} \nabla_{e_k} e_i, e_l) \\ &= (\nabla_{e_k} e_i, \nabla_{e_l} e_j) - (\nabla_{e_k} e_j, \nabla_{e_l} e_i) + \partial_i (g(\nabla_{e_k} e_j, e_l)) - \partial_j(g(\nabla_{e_k} e_i, e_l)) \\ &= -(\nabla_l\nabla_k e_i, e_j) + (\nabla_k \nabla_l e_i, e_j) + [\partial_i(g(\nabla_k e_j, e_l)) - \partial_j(g(\nabla_k e_i, e_l)) \\ &\quad - \partial_k(g(\nabla_i e_l, e_j)) + \partial_l(g(\nabla_k e_i, e_j))] \\ &= R_{klij} + 0 & \text{Last four terms sum to 0}\\ \end{align}

Bianchi identity

\begin{align} \text{First Bianchi Identity:} \quad S := \quad R_{ijkl} + R_{jkil} + R_{kijl} &= 0 \\ R_{ijkl} &= (\partial_i \Gamma_{jl}^k - \partial_j \Gamma_{il}^k) & \text{normal coord.} \\ &= \frac{1}{2}(\partial_i\partial_j g_{kl} + \partial_i\partial_l g_{kj} - \partial_i \partial_k g_{lj}) \\ &\quad - \frac{1}{2}(\partial_j \partial_i g_{lk} + \partial_j \partial_l g_{kl} - \partial_j \partial_k g_{il}) \end{align} Expanding \(R_{jkil}, R_{kijl}\) and summing them will return 0. \begin{align} \text{Second Bianchi Identity:}\quad R_{ijkl,h} + R_{ijlh,k} + R_{ijhk,l} &= 0 \\ \text{In normal coordinates:} \\ R_{ijkl,h} &= \frac{1}{2} (\partial_h\partial_i\partial_l g_{kj} - \partial_h \partial_i\partial_k g_{lj}) \\ &\quad - \frac{1}{2} (\partial_h \partial_j \partial_i g_{kl} - \partial_h \partial_j \partial_k g_{il} )\\ R_{ijlh,k} &= \frac{1}{2} (\partial_k\partial_i\partial_h g_{lj} - \partial_k \partial_i\partial_l g_{hj}) \\ &\quad - \frac{1}{2} (\partial_k \partial_j \partial_i g_{lh} - \partial_k \partial_j \partial_l g_{ih} )\\ R_{ijhk,l} &= \frac{1}{2} (\partial_l\partial_i\partial_k g_{hj} - \partial_l \partial_i\partial_h g_{kj}) \\ &\quad - \frac{1}{2} (\partial_l \partial_j \partial_i g_{hk} - \partial_l \partial_j \partial_h g_{ik} ) \end{align}
