Some Proofs on Lie Groups and Lie algebra

Left Invariant Vector Fields

Suppose $X$ is a left-invariant vector field, and $\phi^X_t$ is its generating flow, such that $\frac{d \phi^X_t(p)}{dt} =X(p)$. Then $\phi^X_t(p) = p\exp(tX_e)$

Proof: \begin{align} L_{p^{-1},*} \frac{d\phi^X_t(p)}{dt}|_{t=0} &= L_{p^{-1}, *} X_{p} = L_{p^{-1}, *} L_{p, *} X_e = X_e \\ \frac{d p^{-1} \phi_t^X(p)}{dt}|_{t=0} &= X_e \end{align} Assume $p^{-1} \phi^X_t(p) = \gamma(t)$. Then, $\gamma(0)=e, \gamma'(0)=X_e$ and \begin{align} \frac{d \gamma(t)}{dt}|_{t=\tau} &= L_{p^{-1},*}X_{\phi^X_t(p)} = L_{p^{-1}\phi^X_t(p),*}X_{e} = L_{\gamma(t), *}(X_e) \end{align} This indicates that $\gamma(t)=\exp(tX_e)$. Thus, \begin{align} \phi^X_t(p) = p\exp(tX_e) \label{eq:1} \end{align}

Lie bracket formulation

Let $\phi^X_t(p), \phi^Y_t(p)$ be the generating flow of the left invariant vector fields $X, Y$ respectively. Then, the canonical definition of the Lie bracket is \begin{align} [X, Y]_p = \frac{d \phi^{X}_{-t, *}Y}{dt} &= \lim_{t \rightarrow 0}\frac{\phi^X_{-t,*} Y_{\phi^{t}_X(p)} - Y_{p}}{t} \\ &= \lim_{t\rightarrow 0} \frac{\phi^X_{-t,*}L_{p \exp(tX_e), *} Y_{e} - L_{p,*}Y_{e}}{t} \\ &= \lim_{t\rightarrow 0} \frac{(\phi^X_{-t} \circ L_{p} \circ L_{\exp(tX_e)})_* Y_{e} - L_{p,*}Y_{e}}{t} \\ \end{align} However, as we know, from \ref{eq:1} that \begin{align} \phi^X_{-t} (L_p L_{\exp(tX_e)} g) = p\exp(tX_e)g\exp(-tX_e) \end{align}
