Representation theory primer

Representation

Definition:

Let $\rho$ be a homomorphism between some group $G$ to a general linear group $GL(V)$, $V$ being some vector space over a field $F$. This is how we refer to as a representation of the group $G$.

A linear subspace $W \subset V$ is called G-invariant if $\rho(g)w \in W$ for all $g\in G$ and all $w\in W$. This restricts $\rho$ to group operation in $W \subset V$, which is also known as the sub-representation of $V$. $\rho$ is called irreducible if it has only trivial subrepresentations.

Given a basis, we can represent a representation as a square matrix $D$. If $D$ can be represented in an upper triangular block by an invertible matrix $P$ (i.e. $D'(g), g \in G$ is block diagonal, $D'(g)=P^{-1}D(g) P$), it is reducible and vice versa.

Proof: Trivial

Any representation is decomposable into irreducible representations. In other words, $P^{-1} D(g) P = D^{(1)}(g) \oplus D^{(2)}(g) \oplus \cdots \oplus D^{(k)}(g)$

Proof: Trivial
